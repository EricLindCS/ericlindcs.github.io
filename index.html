About Me

Hi! I'm Eric


I am a dedicated and passionate Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS) student at the University of California, Berkeley, with a strong interest in machine learning, robotics, and embedded systems. My academic focus is on understanding the intersections of hardware and software to develop innovative solutions that drive technological advancements in AI and IoT devices. In addition to my coursework, I am actively involved in research projects related to data pipelines and processing for theoretical physics. Through hands-on projects, internships, and team collaborations, I have honed my skills in programming languages such as Python, C++, and Java, as well as working with hardware like Arduino microcontrollers and FPGAs. When I'm not immersed in my studies, I enjoy reading, hiking, making small QOL tools for others, participating in hackathons, and volunteering in STEM outreach programs to inspire future generations of engineers. My goal is to leverage my skills and knowledge to contribute to cutting-edge innovation and help develop technology that positively impacts society.
